Most vendors compare agents on commission rates and valuations alone. Those two numbers tell you almost nothing about who will actually perform. A great agent answers every one of these without hesitation.

The Six Questions
01
Strategy
What is your strategy to attract buyers who will pay a premium — not just any buyer?

This separates agents with a genuine marketing plan from those who simply list and wait. A strong agent knows exactly who the buyer is and how to reach them before the campaign even launches.

02
Pricing
How do you determine the right price — and what happens if you get it wrong?

Forces transparency on pricing strategy and exposes agents who inflate valuations to win your listing. An honest answer is a very good sign. Discomfort or deflection is a red flag you should take seriously.

03
Proof
Can you show me evidence of properties you've sold above asking price in this suburb?

Moves the conversation from claims to proof. Any agent can promise a strong result — very few can demonstrate one. Ask for specific addresses, verified sale prices, and what the vendor's expectations were going in.

04
Communication
How often will I hear from you once we're on the market — and what will you tell me?

Vendors fear being ignored after signing. This reveals whether the agent has a real communication system or just good intentions. You should leave knowing exactly what to expect week to week.

05
Execution
What do you do differently in the first 7 days on market — and why does that window matter most?

The first week is when buyer interest peaks and your negotiating leverage is highest. A great agent has a deliberate plan for this window. A weak one gives a vague answer about open homes and hopes for the best.

06
Accountability
If I'm not happy with how things are going — what are my options?

A confident agent answers this without flinching. It shows they back their performance and respect that you have rights as a vendor. Hesitation or deflection here tells you everything before you sign anything.
