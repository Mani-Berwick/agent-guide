Before you choose an agent, ask these six questions.

Selling your home is one of the biggest financial decisions you'll make. The right agent protects that outcome — the wrong one quietly costs you.

"Most people compare agents on the price they quote and the fee they charge. Neither tells you who will actually perform when it counts."

The Six Questions
01Price

"How did you arrive at that price — and can you show me the recent sales behind it?"

The price an agent quotes should come from real, recent sales of homes like yours — not the number they think you want to hear. Agents who quote high to win your business often spend the next month talking you back down. Ask to see the evidence.

02Strategy

"What's your plan to get me the highest price — not just a sale?"

Almost any agent can find a buyer. Getting you the most takes a deliberate strategy for creating competition between buyers and making your home the one they chase. A sale and a great sale are very different results.

03Marketing

"What's your marketing plan, and how does each part actually help sell my home?"

Marketing is what puts your home in front of buyers — and more buyers means more competition and a stronger price. You should understand exactly what you're paying for and why it matters. The cheapest campaign isn't a saving if fewer people ever see your home.

04Negotiation

"When the offers come in, how do you negotiate to push the price higher?"

Your final price is won or lost in the negotiation — and that's where the gap between an average agent and a great one is widest. You want someone who works every offer to your advantage, not someone who simply passes them on and nudges you to accept the first one.

05Communication

"How — and how often — will you keep me updated through the campaign?"

Being left in the dark is the most common regret sellers have. You deserve straight, regular communication — including the honest feedback that's hard to hear. How an agent talks to you in the easy weeks tells you how they'll handle the tough ones.

06Track record

"What have you sold around here recently, and how did the result compare to expectations?"

Local, recent results tell you more than any promise. Ask what's sold, how long it took, and how the final price compared to the asking — and ask to speak with a past seller or two. A confident agent will happily show you.
