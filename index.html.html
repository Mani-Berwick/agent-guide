Selling your home is one of the biggest financial decisions you will make. The agent you choose will either protect that outcome — or cost you.

Most homeowners compare agents on commission rates and valuations alone. Those two numbers tell you almost nothing about who will actually perform. These six questions reveal the real agent behind the pitch.

The Six Questions to Ask Every Agent
01
Strategy

What is your strategy to attract buyers who will pay a premium — not just any buyer?

This separates agents with a genuine marketing plan from those who simply list and wait. A strong agent knows exactly who the buyer is and how to reach them before the campaign launches.

02
Pricing

How do you determine the right price — and what happens if you get it wrong?

Forces transparency on pricing strategy and exposes agents who inflate valuations to win your listing. An honest answer here is a very good sign. Discomfort is a red flag.

03
Proof

Can you show me evidence of properties you've sold above asking price in this suburb?

Moves the conversation from claims to proof. Any agent can promise a strong result — very few can demonstrate one. Ask for specific addresses and verified outcomes.

04
Communication

How often will I hear from you once we're on the market — and what will you tell me?

Vendors fear being ignored after signing. This question reveals whether the agent has a real communication system or just good intentions. You should know exactly what to expect.

05
Execution

What do you do differently in the first 7 days on market — and why does that period matter most?

The first week sets the tone for the entire campaign. A great agent understands buyer psychology and campaign momentum. A weak one will give you a vague answer about open homes.

06
Accountability

If I'm not happy with how things are going, what are my options?

A confident agent answers this without flinching. It shows they back themselves and respect your position. Hesitation or deflection tells you everything you need to know.
